# Accessibility Baseline

Use these checks as non-negotiable defaults when defining design rules.

## Contrast

- Body text should meet WCAG AA contrast.
- Large text may use the lower large-text threshold only when it truly qualifies as large text.
- Interactive boundaries, icons, and focus indicators should also maintain visible contrast.

## Interaction states

- Define default, hover, focus, pressed, disabled, and invalid states where relevant.
- Make focus treatment visually obvious and consistent across components.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate status or selection.

## Sizing and targets

- Use touch-friendly targets for interactive controls.
- Avoid dense icon-only actions without adequate hit area.

## Reading and forms

- Preserve clear label-to-field relationships.
- Distinguish helper text, error text, and success text through more than color.
- Keep form groups visually and structurally coherent.

## Source basis

These rules are based on WCAG 2.2 design-relevant requirements, especially contrast, non-text contrast, reflow, target size, and focus appearance.
